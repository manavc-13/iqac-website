Policies & Guidelines

Our framework for ensuring quality, transparency, and continuous improvement in all institutional processes.

Quality Policy

KIIT Deemed to be University is committed to establishing and maintaining the highest standards of quality in education, research, and extension activities. Our Quality Policy is centered on:

  • Fostering a culture of continuous improvement and innovation in all academic and administrative processes.
  • Ensuring stakeholder satisfaction by meeting their expectations through quality services and outcomes.
  • Adhering to national and international quality benchmarks and best practices in higher education.
  • Promoting ethical practices, transparency, and accountability in all institutional endeavors.
  • Empowering faculty and staff through capacity building and professional development initiatives.
  • Creating a learner-centric environment that nurtures holistic development and global competencies.

Teaching-Learning Policy

KIIT is dedicated to providing a dynamic and effective teaching-learning environment. Our policy emphasizes:

  • Adoption of innovative pedagogical practices, including ICT integration, blended learning, and experiential learning.
  • Regular curriculum review and updation to align with industry needs, global trends, and societal relevance.
  • Continuous assessment and evaluation mechanisms to monitor student progress and provide timely feedback.
  • Encouraging critical thinking, problem-solving, and research aptitude among students.
  • Providing academic support and mentoring to cater to diverse learning needs.
  • Ensuring academic freedom and fostering an environment of intellectual curiosity and scholarly exchange.

Feedback Policy

KIIT values feedback from all its stakeholders as a crucial input for quality enhancement. Our Feedback Policy aims to:

  • Establish a systematic and structured mechanism for collecting feedback from students, faculty, alumni, parents, and employers.
  • Ensure anonymity and confidentiality of respondents to encourage candid feedback.
  • Regularly analyze feedback to identify areas of strength and opportunities for improvement.
  • Communicate the analysis of feedback and action taken reports to relevant stakeholders.
  • Utilize feedback for curriculum development, improvement of services, and overall institutional development.
  • Integrate feedback into the strategic planning and decision-making processes of the university.

Outcome-Based Education (OBE)

KIIT embraces Outcome-Based Education (OBE) to ensure that our programs are student-centric and produce graduates with defined competencies. Our OBE framework focuses on:

  • Clearly defining Program Outcomes (POs), Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs), and Course Outcomes (COs) for all academic programs.
  • Designing curriculum, teaching methodologies, and assessment strategies aligned with these defined outcomes.
  • Implementing robust mechanisms for measuring the attainment of outcomes at course, program, and institutional levels.
  • Utilizing outcome attainment data for continuous improvement of curriculum, pedagogy, and assessment methods.
  • Ensuring that graduates are equipped with the knowledge, skills, and attitudes required for professional success and lifelong learning.
  • Mapping COs to POs/PSOs to ensure comprehensive coverage and attainment of desired graduate attributes.
